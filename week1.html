Study Abroad — Week 1 (ish) Reflection

Overview of August 17 - August 27, 2025

  • Main theme of the week: keep trying new things
  • Biggest highlight: meeting new friends through others and exploring the city
  • Biggest challenge: My assumptions about other americans

More detailed thoughts

  • Copenhagen Opera Festival: Attended two performances. First was an abbreviated version of Carmen by Bizet. That had an amazing conductor. The second was a story about if Jesus was a woman and made me really think about the world which was cool. Also at this cool young hip venue where I could see myself in ten years in the audience members.
  • A LOT of WALKING — Toured Norrebro, walked around the lakes, saw a double rainbow on my path to pick up a too good to go from Lagkahuset.
  • Mixed frisbee practice with Hucks. I love frisbee and the community. Everyone's so willing to learn and teach in a mostly kind way. But I do enjoy mixed more as I feel that there are more chances taken and less precision which slows the game.
  • Nightlife: went to Proud Mary's on a Tuesday for "American Night". Not my scene, take your frat party and add more BO, a professional bar, and more tables to dance on, but I'm glad I saw what it was.
  • Explored the canals. Organized new friends to take out a GoBoat and cruised on the canals while sipping fruit wine, eating cheese, and making dumb jokes. It was a blast!
  • Class. I'm enjoying all of my classes so far, and currently I like my Anthropology of Food class the best because we got to eat! and the professor is quite "bitchy and opinionated" (his words, not mine).

Quick Stats

  • Miles walked: 72!
  • Places visited: Norrebro, The Lakes, Vesterbro, Inner City, Reffen.
  • Favorite food: Onigiri from Come in the meatpacking district
  • Shout out to the new people I met (who I want to hang out with again): Zoe, Sasha, Bea, Ever, Kiara, Mickey, Ariella, Paige, Miles, Molly, Anie, Julia, Clara, Aashna, & Fiona.
